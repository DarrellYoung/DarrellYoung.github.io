Short Academic Bio

Dr. Darrell L. Young, P.E. is a Research Professor at the Southern Methodist University Darwin Deason Institute for Cyber Security (SMU-DDI) and an Adjunct Professor in the SMU Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering. He is a former Raytheon Principal Technical Fellow, an IEEE Senior Member, a member of the IEEE Aerospace and Electronic Systems Society (21 years), IEEE Circuits and Systems Society, and IEEE Communications Society, and a Life Member of SPIE and USGIF. He previously served as Secretary of the Northern Virginia Chapter of IEEE AESS and has been an IEEE member for 36 years. His work focuses on AI-enabled mission systems, resilient edge computing, contested communications, sensing, autonomy, quantum and zero-knowledge proof architectures, and secure operational systems for defense and other high-consequence environments.
